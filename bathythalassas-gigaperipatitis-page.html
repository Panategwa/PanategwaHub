Bathythalassas gigaperipatitis
(Deep-Sea Walker)

Physical Appearance

  • Size & Weight: Juvenile: 50–500 m tall, 500–50,000 tons; Adult: 2–5 km tall, massive hydrostatic body supporting trillions of kg
  • Other Features: Bioluminescent lures, enormous body cavities for feeding, colossal deep-sea physiology

Habitat & Population

  • Habitat: Deep oceans — abyssal plains, trenches, mid-ocean ridges; rarely shallow waters
  • Population: Extremely low — dozens to hundreds due to size and resource needs

Diet & Reproduction

  • Diet: Filter-feeder and opportunistic predator; absorbs plankton, krill, and deep-sea creatures through massive body cavities
  • Reproduction: Releases drifting spores that float for centuries before settling; lifespan may exceed 10,000 years

Behavior & Ecology

  • Behavior: Extremely slow-moving, carried by currents; passive toward most species but territorial in feeding zones; acts as a mobile ecosystem supporting thousands of species
  • Ecological Role: Apex organism of the deep sea; shapes currents, provides habitat, regulates prey populations, inspires myths
  • Territorial Behavior: Occupies vast territories spanning hundreds of kilometers; resting areas in deep trenches or underwater mountains
