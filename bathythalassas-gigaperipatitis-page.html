Bathythalassas gigaperipatitis
(Deep-Sea Colossus)

Overview:
Colossal deep-sea species of Panategwa d, dominating the abyssal zones and influencing entire ocean ecosystems.
Habitat:
Deep oceans, especially abyssal plains, trenches, and mid-ocean ridges. Rarely approaches shallow waters.
Population:
Extremely low — estimated in the dozens to hundreds due to immense size and resource needs.
Size & Weight:
Juvenile: 50–500 m tall, 500–50,000 tons
Adult: 2–5 km tall (1.2–3.1 mi), massive hydrostatic body supporting trillions of kg
Diet:
Filter-feeder and opportunistic predator; absorbs plankton, krill, and deep-sea creatures through massive body cavities and bioluminescent lures.
Reproduction:
Releases enormous drifting spores that float for centuries before settling to grow into juveniles. Lifespan may exceed 10,000 years.
Behavior:
Extremely slow-moving, carried by currents. Passive toward most species but territorial in feeding zones. Acts as a mobile ecosystem supporting thousands of other species.

Apex organism of the deep sea; shapes currents, provides habitat, regulates prey populations, and inspires myths among .
Territorial Behavior:
Occupies vast territories spanning hundreds of kilometers; resting areas are deep trenches or underwater mountains.
