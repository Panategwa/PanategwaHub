The Dendrospheres of Panategwa e

Overview

  • Planet: Mostly frozen, average surface temperature of –200 °C.
  • Dendrospheres: Huge forest areas made of trees several kilometers tall.
  • Temperature: Inside, it can reach 100 °C, creating warm pockets in the icy planet.

Size and Spread

  • Number: About 30,000–50,000 dendrospheres across the planet.
  • Size: Usually 50–200 km wide.
  • Tree count: 10–40 million trees per dendrosphere.

Roots and Networks

  • Depth: Roots can reach 5–10 kilometers underground, absorbing heat from the planet’s interior.
  • Ice melting: Underground heat melts nearby ice into water, which is drawn up to feed rivers, lakes, and wetlands inside the forest.
  • Tree and root network: Roots are connected, sharing energy, nutrients, and chemical signals so that no tree dies from lack of resources.
  • Forest teamwork: The entire dendrosphere acts like a single living system due to its root network.

Inside the Dendrospheres

  • Air: Humid and layered under the thick canopy of trees.
  • Water: Rivers, lakes, and thermal wetlands from melted ice.
  • Life: Plants and animals are adapted to heat, crowded spaces, and low light.

Importance

  • Biodiversity: Most of Panategwa e’s life lives inside dendrospheres.
  • Climate: Keeps the forest warm and stops ice from taking over nearby areas.
  • Habitat: Almost all species cannot survive outside these forests.
