Thrinsachelom History

Origins

All Thrinsacheloms descend from a single subspecies called Anthropoi civilis rashtonus, which originally only inhabited the Rashootian Isles. These early Thrinsacheloms lived in small, isolated tribes.

Over long periods of migration, groups spread outward. Some settled in Pitosia, Arifatonia, and other regions. This geographic isolation eventually led to divergence, forming three major subspecies: Rashtonus, Pitosianus, and Arifatonus. Although they are still biologically compatible, they developed distinct features, such as physical traits, languages, and cultures.

For tens of thousands of years, the world remained fragmented into thousands of tribes, they lived, they fought, and they disappeared, without ever knowing how vast their world truly was...

The Number 18

Thrinsacheloms possess six fingers on each hand, a feature that shaped their mathematical thinking and culture. This number, multiplied by their three major subspecies, made the number 18 emerge as a natural symbol of balance and completion.

Over time, 18 became deeply embedded in their traditions. It represented order and perfection. Systems of leadership, timekeeping, and even warfare were often structured around it. To the Thrinsacheloms, 18 was not just a number, it was a reflection of how the world was meant to function.

The First Leviathan Encounter(124 B.G.W.)

A major turning point came when a young Leviathan separated from its pack appeared along the coast of a tribe known as the Rashooties, and it destroyed many coastal settlements. For the first time in history, separate tribes united out of necessity rather than choice.

After a 3-year struggle, the creature was killed. Its death marked the first recorded instance of large-scale cooperation between tribes and demonstrated the potential of unified action.

The Rise of Rashooti and the Great Food(121 B.G.W.)

During the aftermath of the leviathan encounter, the surrounding tribes held a huge feast, and consumed the Leviathan. Within it was a dense, nutrient-rich sac containing a substance later known as The Great Food.

The tribal leader Rashooti consumed large amounts of this substance. Over time, he grew physically larger, stronger, and more intelligent. His lifespan also extended far beyond normal limits.

Recognizing its power, Rashooti secured control of the remaining supply and used his advantage to unite much of the Rashootian Isles. His rule marked the first major centralized authority in Thrinsachelom history.

The Rise of Pitos(110 B.G.W.)

On the continent of Pitosia, a leader named Pitos rose to power through strategy, organization, and relentless expansion. His forces conquered neighboring tribes and expanded into Arifatonia, enslaving large populations.

However, his expansion faced serious resistance. The Jamuhairies, known for their strength and deep loyalty, formed alliances with the Rashooties. Their combined influence made them one of the few forces capable of opposing Pitos.

To avoid direct conflict with them, Pitos shifted strategy. While continuing to conquer weaker regions, he began sending valuable gifts and tribute to Rashooti. Over time, this created a fragile alliance.

Rashooti eventually shared portions of the Great Food with Pitos. This changed everything. With enhanced strength, intelligence, and lifespan, Pitos accelerated his conquests and extended influence across most of the known world.

At its peak, Pitosian control resembled a vast empire. Entire populations were enslaved, cultures were reshaped, and resistance was systematically crushed.

The Great Meeting(1 B.G.W.)

As Rashooti grew stronger in the Rashootian Isles, and Pitos expanded across Pitosia and Arifatonia, the world became unstable. Small tribes could no longer stay isolated, because trade, war, and alliances connected everything.

The Great Food became the most important resource in the world. It could make leaders stronger, smarter, and live longer. Because of this, it changed the balance of power across the entire world.

To stop full global war, leaders from all major regions gathered in the Great Meeting. It was the biggest meeting of leaders in history.

The meeting lasted a long time, with many arguments and broken deals. Some wanted free access to the Great Food, but most refused.

In the end, they agreed on a plan. The Great Food would only be given to a small number of leaders. But first, leadership had to be reduced through controlled conflict.

A preparation time of 1296 days was given before this war began.

The Start of the Great War(0 B.G.W.)

After the preparation time ended, the Great War began. It was not one single battle, but a global system of conflict.

The goal was to reduce all leadership across the world down to exactly 18 leaders. This number was seen as balanced and important.

The war lasted exactly 1296 sunsets.

At first, many small wars started everywhere at the same time. Tribes fought, formed alliances, and broke them quickly.

As time passed, smaller tribes were absorbed into larger groups. Only strong and organized leaders remained active.

By the end, almost all small tribes were gone, and only major leaders were left fighting for survival.

Jamuhairi and Jamuhali (During the Great War)(0 B.G.W.)

Near the end of the Great War, two important leaders remained: Jamuhairi and his younger brother Jamuhali.

Jamuhali was ready to fight. He believed only one of them could survive.

Jamuhairi refused to fight directly. Instead, he avoided final battles and tried to delay conflict for as long as possible.

Because of this, their war became slow and unfinished. Their armies fought small battles, but nothing decisive ever happened.

As the final sunset arrived, the Great War ended, but Jamuhairi and Jamuhali were still both alive.

The entire world was tired of the war, so they agreed to let both brothers become leaders.

So both were allowed to stay alive.

This changed the final count to 19 leaders instead of 18. It also made Jamuhairies known for strong family loyalty.

The Pitosian Resistance(11 A.G.W.)

After the war, the Pitosian system stayed the strongest power in the world. But many regions did not accept their control.

Small rebellions started in many places. Some lasted for a short time, others lasted longer.

Most of them were defeated and taken back under control. But the constant fighting made both sides weaker over time.

Because of this, leaders later met again and agreed to reduce large-scale wars in the future.

Small conflicts still existed, but global war became rare.

The Pitosian Calendar

The end of the Great War marked the beginning of recorded time. From this point forward, most civilizations adopted the Pitosian calendar.

A year consists of 18 months, each containing 12 weeks, with 6 days per week, for a total of 1296 days.

This structure reflects the importance of the number 18 and reinforces its role as a symbol of balance and order within Thrinsachelom society.
